Mission


Welcome, and thank you for visiting.

Sen Translation is motivated by my belief that translation is a craft where in-depth domain knowledge, linguistic skill, and understanding of client needs is paramount. These days, getting a translation often means working with large agencies who increasingly use AI translation followed by low-cost human editing, a process which sacrifices on all of these key factors. You'll be forced to communicate via a middleman and won't know your translator (nor whether they're even a human). In this system, neither skilled translators nor clients are truly respected.

Clients don't deserve the haphazard, good-enough approach of many agencies. In response to this, I not only employ my years worth of scientific, technical, and linguistic expertise, but I also seek to deeply understand client needs so that each translation is purposefully crafted. I ask questions, perform in-depth research, keep you consistently informed throughout the process, and never hand your data over to AI. For a truly high-quality translation, I believe there is simply no better way.

I highly look forward to working together and delivering the translation you deserve.

Sincerely,

Translation Process

Step 1: Initial Reading + Quote
Send me an email with a description and scan of the document(s) you need translated. I will review this to determine the amount of time/cost needed to complete the project and deliver a quote to you.

Step 2: Research + Draft
After you accept the quote, I will research any background knowledge necessary for delivering a translation that is accurate and natural. I then perform the translation myself, without exposing your data to AI systems or any outside parties.

Step 3: Revision(s)
I perform several rounds of checks and send you a sample of the completed translation. If you have any required changes, I will make revisions as necessary. This process may be repeated several times.

Step 4: Final Delivery
After agreeing on a final version, I will deliver it to you in whichever format you require. I will send you an invoice and request payment within 30 days.

1. Quotes, Fees, Payment

Q: What currencies do you accept?
A: I accept payment in USD, JPY, and NTD.

Q: How do I receive a quote?
A: Please send me an email at devin@sen-translation.com with details about your request. After discussing the details, I can issue a quote to you.

Q: How much will it cost?
A: Each project is different, but I ask a minimum of $35.00, which covers about 250 words. Beyond that, cost depends mostly on the amount of content, the type of content, and the deadline. If you require a project to be completed within 1-3 days or over a weekend, this may incur a rush fee. To receive a quote, please read more here.

Q: Why a minimum fee?
A: No matter the length of your text, I ask a minimum fee of $35.00, which covers up to roughly 250 words. This charge covers the linguistic and field-specific research work necessary for every project, ensuring that the language of the final product is contextually accurate and natural.

Q: Do you offer refunds?
A: I do not typically offer refunds, but I understand that sometimes mistakes and/or miscommunications can happen. If you experience an issue with the translation received, I will issue a revised version as soon as possible and at no extra charge. Nevertheless, I strive to keep our communication open and frequent in order to avoid such issues.

2. Work Process

Q: How long will it take?
A: This depends on the number of documents and the type of content. Typically, I aim for returning small-medium projects within a week. Overall, I believe it is always better to take sufficient time to ensure quality instead of delivering a rushed product that may not meet your standards.

Q: Do you provide certified translations?
A: In the US, there is no official, legal process for certification. However, upon request, I am happy to attach a statement of certification with my signature to any translations I perform. This statement serves to show that I take responsibility for the accuracy of the translation performed.

Q: Do you also translate from English into Japanese and Taiwanese Mandarin?
A: I only translate into my native language, English-US. Translating into a language other than one's native language can be risky, as a non-native speaker does not have the same intuitions for naturalness as a native speaker. There are some bilingual translators who offer such service, but in the interest of my own high quality, I do not.

Q: Do you use computer-aided translation (CAT) tools?
A: In general, no. I find such tools can get in the way of fully understanding and working directly with the full document(s). However, if it is requested by a client, I may use Trados or MemoQ.

Q: Do you use AI?
A: Absolutely not, and I advise you to be careful with providers who do use such systems. For one, uploading your data to such systems constitutes a breach of privacy as your data is given to a third party beyond the translator. Furthermore, there is little regulation on how your data will be used. Secondly, translation is a highly technical skill which requires a high level of subject-matter expertise and contextual knowledge. Machine translation systems are, by design, simply making statistical guesses over pieces of text without any such expertise or knowledge. I believe clients deserve better than mere guesses, and working directly with a human allows you to have your input heard in the process.

Q: Do you work with translation agencies?
A: I consider work with agencies if they have a record of high quality, pay their translators fairly, and do not use AI (i.e., I do not accept MTPE work).

Q: How is my data stored?
A: I take data privacy very seriously. Whatever documents you send to me will be stored offline, under password protection in accordance with NIST guidelines. For any software I use, I turn off any services that connect your data to third parties (i.e., AI training, etc.)
