Frequently Asked Questions

Languages & Scope

Can I discuss my project before requesting a quote?

Certainly. If you want to discuss your translation needs before committing, feel free to contact me via my contact form or schedule a call.

Do you also translate from English into other languages?

As a general rule, it is best for translators to translate into their native language to ensure highest quality. Therefore, I only translate into my native English.

Do you provide certified translations?

This depends what you mean by "certified". For example, in the United States, there is no recognized legal process for certification. However, upon request, I am happy to attach a statement of responsibility and accuracy with my signature.

Workflow

Can you follow a style guide or preferred terminology?

Absolutely. If you have existing glossaries, prior translations, internal style guides, or preferred terminology lists, I can follow them to maintain consistency with your standards.

Can you translate figures, tables, and embedded text in images?

Yes, in many cases I can handle tables, figures, and embedded text, though this may depend on file quality and editability. If significant formatting work is required, additional charges may be applied.

Do you use AI or machine translation tools?

In general, no. AI tools often introduce more issues than they solve. Importantly, they cannot ensure understanding of the underlying technical pieces, choose appropriate wording when it matters, and maintain consistency.

How do you handle confidentiality?

I treat all documents as confidential and use secure file handling practices. I am also happy to review and sign a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) when needed.

Workflow & Delivery

Can you provide partial delivery for long projects?

Yes. For larger projects, I can deliver in stages so you can begin review and downstream work earlier while I continue on the remaining sections.

How do I review the translation? Can I request revisions?

I provide the translated document in your preferred format. You can review it and share feedback for any revisions or clarifications. I will work with you to ensure the final translation meets your needs.

How fast can you work?

This depends highly on length, technical complexity, and current schedule. I can usually offer standard and expedited options after reviewing your document.

How is pricing determined?

Pricing is based on factors such as length, technical complexity, formatting requirements, and turnaround expectations.

What file formats do you accept and return?

I can work with common office formats such as PDF, DOCX, PPTX, and plain text files. I return the translation to you in the format you prefer, as long as it is possible. Other file types may incur additional cost if the text/formatting cannot be easily extracted.

Frequently Asked Questions

one-on-one Advising

Can you review presentations before a session?

Yes. If you share slides, notes, or a draft talk in advance, I can give targeted feedback on content, structure, and delivery.

What happens in a writing session?

We focus on a concrete challenge: outlining a section, refining argument structure, clarifying key claims, or revising problem areas in a draft.

What if I am not sure which kind of advising I need?

That is exactly what the flexible option is for. If your needs do not fit neatly into one category, we can decide together what kind of support would be most useful.

Editing

Can you focus on argument flow and clarity, not just grammar?

Yes. A major part of my work is helping strengthen logical flow, section organization, and reader comprehension so your key points are easy to follow.

What is the difference between proofreading, copyediting, and deep editing?

Proofreading addresses grammar, spelling, punctuation, and surface-level consistency. Copyediting improves clarity, sentence flow, and style while preserving meaning. Deep editing involves heavier revision for overall structure, argument strength, and coherence across sections.

What types of documents do you work on?

I work on documents such as journal manuscripts, dissertations and theses, conference papers, grant proposals, technical reports, and presentation materials.

Delivery

Can you help with typesetting and writing platforms? (LaTeX, etc.)

Yes. I can work with typesetting and writing workflows using LaTeX, Typst, and platforms such as Overleaf.

Can you help with urgent deadlines and what turnaround times do you offer?

Turnaround depends on length, complexity, and current schedule. I can usually offer standard and expedited options after reviewing your document. If you have a tight deadline, contact me with your word count and target date, and I will let you know what is feasible.

Do you keep documents confidential?

Yes. I treat all materials as confidential and can review and sign NDAs when needed.

How do you return edits?

When possible, I return files with tracked changes and comments so you can review each revision clearly and decide what to accept. I can use software such as Word, Google Docs, Overleaf, etc.

How is pricing determined?

Pricing is based on scope, document complexity, turnaround expectations, and whether the work is editing or one-on-one advising.
